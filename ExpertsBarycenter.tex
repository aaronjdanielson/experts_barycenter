\begin{abstract}
We study the aggregation of expert probability vectors over a finite outcome space.
The standard approach, the arithmetic mean (linear opinion pool), is sensitive to extreme
opinions: a single expert who concentrates mass on a tail outcome shifts the aggregate
by the same amount regardless of how far that expert sits from the consensus.
We propose the Wasserstein barycenter as a robust alternative.
Our main result shows that under the indicator ground cost, the Wasserstein barycenter
reduces to a constrained $\ell^1$ aggregation problem, making computation tractable and
robustness transparent.
For ordered outcome spaces, which are the natural setting for histogram probability forecasts,
the Wasserstein-1 barycenter has a fully closed-form solution: it is the distribution
whose cumulative distribution function is the component-wise median of the individual
expert CDFs.
We verify this formula numerically to machine precision in an application to the
Survey of Professional Forecasters (SPF): for 2022:Q1 core CPI histogram forecasts,
the barycenter concentrates mass at 2--4\% inflation and assigns zero probability to
the tail bins (deflation and $\geq\!4\%$) that a minority of extreme forecasters
pull into the arithmetic mean.
A second application to a public health expert survey illustrates the unordered case.
Simulation evidence confirms that the barycenter is more robust to outlying opinions
than the arithmetic mean at an efficiency cost of 1.4--1.9 in mean squared error
under symmetric Dirichlet populations ($N=5$--$20$), with the ratio rising
toward $2.5$ as expert heterogeneity increases, consistent with the known tradeoff between $\ell^1$ and $\ell^2$ estimation.
\end{abstract}
