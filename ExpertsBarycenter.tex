\begin{abstract}
We study the aggregation of expert probability forecasts over a finite outcome space.
The standard approach, the arithmetic mean (linear opinion pool), is simple and widely used but can be sensitive to extreme opinions: a small minority of forecasters assigning substantial mass to tail outcomes can pull the aggregate away from the majority view.
We propose the Wasserstein barycenter as a robust alternative.
Our main result shows that under the indicator ground cost, the barycenter reduces to a constrained $\ell^1$ aggregation problem.
For ordered outcome spaces, the natural setting for histogram forecasts, the Wasserstein-1 barycenter has a closed-form solution: its cumulative distribution function is the component-wise median of the individual forecast CDFs.
This yields an interpretable and easily computable robust aggregator for probability histograms.
In an application to the 2022:Q1 Survey of Professional Forecasters core CPI histograms, the barycenter concentrates mass on the 2--4\% inflation range and assigns zero probability to tail bins that a minority of extreme forecasters pull into the arithmetic mean.
Simulation evidence shows that the barycenter is less sensitive to outlying opinions than the arithmetic mean, at the expected efficiency cost under homogeneous expert populations.
\end{abstract}
